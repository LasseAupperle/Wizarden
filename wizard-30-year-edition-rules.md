# Wizard — 30-Year Edition: Complete Cards & Rules

*A trick-prediction card game by Ken Fisher (illustrations by Franz Vohwinkel & B. Spelger), published by AMIGO. For 3–6 players, ages 10+, ~45 minutes. Based on the official AMIGO rulebook, version 1.1 (2026).*

---

## What's in the box

- **60 character cards** — the standard Wizard deck
- **9 special cards** — Vampire, Witch, Shapeshifter, Dragon, Fairy, Bomb, Werewolf, Juggler, Cloud
- **1 colour card** — used to mark the chosen trump colour when a Wizard sets the trump
- **20 round chips** — track how many rounds you've played
- **Point chips** — for scoring:
  - **54 prediction chips** (their reverse side is worth 10 points each)
  - **36× "20" chips**, **22× "100" chips**, **18× "−10" chips**, **11× "−50" chips**
- **1 start marker** — marks the dealer

---

## The goal

Each round you predict how many tricks you'll win. You only score well if your prediction is exactly right. You play a series of rounds, each with one more card than the last, and whoever has the most experience points at the end wins.

A **trick** is one card played by each player in turn; together those cards form the trick. If everyone holds five cards, you'll play five tricks that round.

---

## The deck explained

The 60 character cards are essentially a themed standard deck:

- **Four colours** (suits), each numbered **1 to 13**. Within the same colour, a higher number beats a lower one.
- **4 Wizards** — the strongest cards. A Wizard belongs to no colour and beats everything.
- **4 Jesters (Narren)** — the weakest cards. A Jester belongs to no colour and loses to everything.

Wizards and Jesters can be played at any time, even when you could follow the led colour.

---

## Setup

1. The "wisest" player takes the **start marker** and deals the first round. The deal and the start marker pass one seat clockwise at the end of every round.
2. Put the **prediction chips**, **point chips**, and **colour card** within reach.
3. Take **round chips** equal to the number of rounds you'll play, depending on player count, and lay them out in an ascending row. Return the rest to the box:

| Players | Rounds played | Round chips used |
|---------|---------------|------------------|
| 3       | 20            | 1–20             |
| 4       | 15            | 1–15             |
| 5       | 12            | 1–12             |
| 6       | 10            | 1–10             |

*(With 3 players you get 20 cards each by the final round; with 6 players you run out of cards sooner, hence fewer rounds.)*

> **Base game vs. extended game:** For your first games, play the **base game** — return all 9 special cards to the box. Once you're comfortable, add the special cards (see "The special cards" below).

---

## How a round works

Every round has four steps: **deal → predict → play → score**.

### 1. Deal the cards (and set trump)

- In round 1, everyone gets **1 card**. In round 2, **2 cards**. In round 3, **3 cards**, and so on.
- Place the undealt cards face-down in a pile to the side. Flip the **top card face-up** — its colour is the **trump colour** for this round. Any card of the trump colour beats any card of a different colour. Trump is set fresh each round.
- **If the flipped card is a Wizard:** the player with the start marker chooses the trump colour. Look at your hand, announce your choice, and slide the colour card under the pile showing that colour.
- **If the flipped card is a Jester:** there is **no trump** this round.
- **The final round** has no trump, because all cards are dealt out and none is left to flip. *(In the extended game with special cards, there are enough extra cards that even the last round still has a card to flip for trump.)*

### 2. Predict your tricks

- Look at your hand and decide how many tricks you'll win this round.
- The player **clockwise of the start marker** predicts first, says their number aloud, and takes that many prediction chips. Everyone else follows clockwise.
- Your prediction chips sit in front of you so everyone can see who's aiming for how many tricks.
- **Predictions do not have to add up to the number of tricks available.** Everyone might bid 2 even if only 3 tricks exist that round, or someone might bid 0.

### 3. Play the tricks

- Whoever made the **first prediction** leads the first card, starting the first trick. Everyone else follows clockwise, one card each.
- **Following suit:** you must play a card of the same colour as the first card led. Only if you have none of that colour may you play a trump or a different colour. The colour to follow never changes within a trick.
- **Wizards and Jesters** may always be played, even if you could follow suit — they belong to no colour.

**Who wins the trick:**

1. The **first Wizard** played in the trick, OR
2. if no Wizard, the **highest trump card**, OR
3. if no trump either, the **highest card of the colour that was led**.

The winner takes the trick's cards face-down in front of them, places one of their **prediction chips on top of the won trick** (so you can see at a glance how many more tricks they still need), and then leads the next trick.

**Special lead situations:**

- **Wizard led first:** everyone else may play any card (even more Wizards or Jesters). The trick always goes to the first Wizard.
- **Jester led first:** the others may play any card *until* someone plays a Wizard or a number card. If it's a **number card**, that colour must be followed from then on. If it's a **Wizard**, everyone may keep playing any card for the rest of the trick.
- **Only Jesters in a trick:** the **first Jester played** wins it.

### 4. Score experience points

- **Correct prediction:** flip all your prediction chips to their point side — each is now worth **10 points**. Then take a **"20" chip** as a bonus for being right. (So a correct bid of *n* tricks = **20 + 10×n** points; a correct bid of 0 = **20** points.)
- **Wrong prediction:** you **lose 10 points for every trick over or under** your bid. Take that many "−10" chips and return all your prediction chips to the supply.
- You may exchange your chips with the supply at any time (e.g. swap ten "10"s for a "100").
- At the end of the round, flip the **lowest remaining round chip** to its back to mark the round as done.

---

## End of the game

The game ends after you've scored the final round and flipped over the last round chip. Whoever has the **most experience points** wins. If several players tie for the most, they win together.

## The special cards (extended game)

New in this 30-year edition: the **Witch** and the **Vampire**. Returning from earlier anniversary editions: the **Shapeshifter, Cloud, Juggler, Werewolf, Bomb, Fairy,** and **Dragon**.

**How to add them:** Shuffle your chosen special cards into the 60 character cards. The special cards give you more options, which makes predicting your tricks harder. To learn, start with just a few:

- The **Shapeshifter** is the easiest to add, so start with it, then bring in the others.
- The **Dragon and Fairy must always be added together** (they balance each other).

A special card may always be played, even when you could follow suit. Each has its own effect:

### Shapeshifter (Gestaltenwandler)
It's either a **Jester or a Wizard** — you decide. When you play it, announce which one it is and play it with that side facing the table; then treat it exactly like that card.
*If flipped to set trump:* you choose the trump colour.

### Dragon (Drache)
The **highest card in the game** — higher than any Wizard, it wins every trick. The **only exception** is when the Dragon and the Fairy are in the same trick (see Fairy).
*If flipped to set trump:* you choose the trump colour.
*If you lead with it:* the same follow rules apply as leading a Wizard.

### Fairy (Fee)
**Lower than a Jester**, so it normally loses every trick. **But if the Dragon and Fairy are in the same trick, the Fairy wins!**
*If flipped to set trump:* there is no trump this round.
*If you lead with it:* the same follow rules apply as leading a Jester.

### Bomb (Bombe)
When played, **nobody wins the trick** — set it aside; it counts toward no one's prediction. Whoever *would* have won (had the Bomb not been played) leads the next trick.
*If flipped to set trump:* there is no trump this round.
*If you lead with it:* the same follow rules apply as leading a Jester.

### Werewolf (Werwolf)
If you hold the Werewolf, then **before predictions begin**, you swap it for the card that was flipped to determine trump. You then choose a trump colour for the round, or declare **"no trump."** Only after this do predictions start.
*If flipped to set trump:* you choose the trump colour.

### Juggler (Jongleur)
Value **7½** (higher than a 7, lower than an 8). When you play it, choose any colour for it (even trump) and announce it. **When the trick ends, everyone simultaneously passes one hand card face-down clockwise**, then picks up the new card.
- If the Bomb is in the trick, you still pass cards.
- If the Juggler is in the **last trick of a round**, you do **not** pass.
*If flipped to set trump:* you choose the trump colour.
*If you lead with it:* the announced colour must be followed.

### Cloud (Wolke)
Value **9¾** (higher than a 9, lower than a 10). When you play it, choose any colour for it (even trump) and announce it. **Whoever wins the trick the Cloud is in must change their own prediction by +1 or −1**, immediately at the end of that trick.
- If the Cloud and Bomb are in the same trick, the prediction does **not** change.
- If the Cloud and Juggler are in the same trick, the **Juggler resolves first** (everyone passes a card left), then the trick winner adjusts their prediction ±1.
*If flipped to set trump:* you choose the trump colour.
*If you lead with it:* the announced colour must be followed.

### Witch (Hexe) — *new*
**Lower than both a Jester and the Fairy.** When you play it, **first resolve all other special-card effects and determine the trick winner.** Then put one of your hand cards into the trick and take any card from the trick into your hand — this may be a special card, but **not the Witch itself**. The card you place into the trick has no effect.
- Because of this, the same card can end up being played more than once in a round.
*If flipped to set trump:* there is no trump this round.
*If you lead with it:* the same follow rules apply as leading a Jester.

### Vampire (Vampir) — *new*
When you play it, it **copies the card that was flipped to determine the trump colour**, including all of that card's effects if it's a special card.
- If the flipped trump card is the **Werewolf**, you immediately flip a new trump card; the new trump applies from the current trick to the end of the round, and the Vampire copies that card and all its effects.
- If you flip a Wizard (or equivalent), you choose the trump colour.
- **Special case:** if someone plays trump and you hold the Vampire, you are **not** forced to follow with it — even though it would copy a trump card.
*If flipped to set trump:* you choose the trump colour.
*If you lead with it:* the follow rules of the copied card apply.

## Optional variants

**Playable with special cards:**

- **Plus/Minus One:** The total of everyone's predictions may **not** equal the number of tricks available that round. (If 5 tricks are possible, the bids must total more or fewer than 5 — guaranteeing at least one player misses.)
- **Hidden Tip:** Everyone takes their prediction chips secretly and reveals them only once all have bid, so no one is influenced by others.
- **Secret Prediction:** Everyone writes their prediction down privately and reveals it only after the round is over.

**Not playable with special cards:**

- **Clairvoyance (Hellsehen):** In round 1 only, hold your single card to your forehead facing out — you see everyone else's card but not your own — then predict. Play and scoring are normal, as are all later rounds.
- **Single Colour (Einfarbig, 3–4 players):** Each player gets all the cards of one colour, including the Wizards and Jesters of that set. With 3 players, remove one colour. There's **no trump**. Shuffle your own pile, draw 4 cards for round 1, and one more each round. Colour is irrelevant — the highest value wins; ties go to the first such card played. Re-sort and return cards by colour after each round, reshuffle, and play **12 rounds**.
- **Wizard Maximus:** Just **5 rounds**, dealing all cards each round. Trump is Blue in round 1, Red in round 2, Green in round 3, Yellow in round 4, and **no trump** in round 5.
